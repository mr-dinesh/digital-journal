Not everyone who talks loudly about "hacking" or collects certifications is worth listening to.
A few markers that hold up:

- **They say "it depends" more than they say "never"** — real threats are contextual; anyone with a one-size answer is selling something.
- **They can explain why, not just what** — knowing a CVE number is trivia; knowing the underlying class of vulnerability is understanding.
- **They're honest about what they don't know** — the field is too wide for any one person to cover; imposters fake confidence, practitioners cite scope.
- **They've broken something or fixed something real** — war stories beat slide decks every time.
